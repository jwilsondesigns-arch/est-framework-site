EST Dictionary

Standard terms translated into EST language

This page provides a compact glossary for readers approaching Elastic Spacetime Theory for the first time. Each term gives a familiar physics label alongside the corresponding EST interpretation.

Spacetime
EST interpretation

Not an abstract passive stage, but a physical hyperelastic medium whose deformation carries physical structure.

Gravity
EST interpretation

Gravity is treated as a large-scale response of the medium to tension gradients and elastic stress, rather than as a separately fundamental interaction.

Particle
EST interpretation

A particle is an extended, stable excitation or defect of the medium rather than an ideal point with unexplained intrinsic properties.

Photon
EST interpretation

A propagating wave mode of the medium, with polarisation arising from structured degrees of freedom of that mode.

Mass
EST interpretation

Mass reflects the confined energy and stable geometric strain structure of a bounded excitation in the medium.

Charge
EST interpretation

Charge corresponds to a persistent directional or topological property of an excitation, not an arbitrary label attached from outside.

Spin
EST interpretation

Spin is interpreted geometrically through internal structured motion, twist, or orientation of a bounded excitation of the medium.

Neutrino
EST interpretation

A topological screw-dislocation-like excitation, giving a geometric route to its weak interaction and mass hierarchy behaviour.

Dark energy
EST interpretation

An emergent large-scale void-pressure effect within the medium, rather than a separate vacuum-energy fluid inserted by hand.

Quantum mechanics
EST interpretation

Quantum behaviour is treated as the constrained mode structure, interference, and threshold response of a real medium, rather than as irreducible mystery.

Void lattice
EST interpretation

Large cosmic voids are treated as structured cells of an elastically organised medium, with a characteristic large-scale spacing as an observable signature.

Repulsive void lensing
EST interpretation

Deep voids are predicted to produce an enhanced negative weak-lensing convergence, appearing as a stronger defocusing signature than standard expectations.

Note: This page is a reader’s guide, not a substitute for the technical papers. Formal derivations and quantitative predictions are given in the EST preprints and supporting notes.
